jelford -> spool

spool a JavaScript component and accompanying UserScript to let your users speed-read web-pages. The quickest way to try it out is to click on the description below.

spool, inspired by Spritz displays text on the screen a single word at a time. The idea is that by aligning the words to the optimal position for a reader's eye, they can recognise the word without having to re-adjust (a movement called a "sacade." There's a lot of reasearch available on the subject of the eye's "optimal landing position," but you can think of it as simply being the place in a word where the eye should rest in order to read it quickly.

To control spool, you can just hover the mouse over the text, and you'll see some controls appear. You can adjust the speed at which words are presented (the "wpm" option), and activate "continuous spool" to have spool automatically move to the next paragraph after the last one finishes. Just hit ■ to stop spool, and click-and-drag the word to the left to rewind (or, to the right to go forward).

If you have Greasemonkey installed, or you're running Chrome and you know how to install UserScripts, you can install the script into your browser and activate it on any web-page with alt+click. You can get it here (more details on GitHub).
